The Agency Reduction Framework

A modern operating framework for reducing agency dependency through internal workforce optimization, predictive scheduling visibility, and automated staffing governance.

Explore Framework
Operational Outcomes

Replacing reactive staffing with operational control

High agency utilization is often a symptom of fragmented scheduling visibility, delayed vacancy response, and limited internal workforce coordination. Modern staffing operations require continuous visibility and automated escalation pathways.

Faster Internal Coverage

Open shifts are surfaced to qualified internal staff before agency escalation occurs, improving fill efficiency and continuity of care.

Predictive Workforce Visibility

Staffing pressure, vacancy trends, absenteeism patterns, and overtime exposure are identified before shortages become operational emergencies.

Reduced Reactive Escalation

External agency staffing becomes a controlled exception rather than the default response to operational instability.

Operational Challenges

Common staffing failure points in LTC operations

Many long-term care facilities experience recurring staffing instability due to fragmented workflows, limited workforce visibility, and reactive scheduling processes.

Last-Minute Sick Calls

Unexpected callouts force schedulers into reactive agency escalation and overtime balancing.

Manual Union Calculations

Seniority rules and collective agreement requirements are often managed manually under time pressure.

Fragmented Scheduling Visibility

Staffing data across units or homes is disconnected, limiting operational coordination.

Reactive Vacancy Management

Staffing shortages are often identified only after scheduling gaps already impact operations.

Strategic Framework

The four-layer agency reduction architecture

Sustainable agency reduction requires operational visibility, workforce flexibility, automated scheduling governance, and proactive staffing coordination working together.

01

Internal Float Optimization

Open shifts are automatically surfaced to available qualified staff before external staffing escalation pathways are triggered.

02

Predictive Scheduling Visibility

Scheduling trends, absenteeism patterns, and vacancy pressure are continuously monitored to identify staffing instability earlier.

03

Union-Aware Assignment Logic

Seniority rules, qualifications, and collective agreement requirements are enforced automatically during assignment workflows.

04

Agency Escalation Governance

External agency utilization becomes structured, measurable, and operationally governed rather than reactive.
