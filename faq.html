Frequently asked questions

Learn how BaseCare helps long-term care and healthcare organizations modernize workforce operations.

What is BaseCare?

BaseCare is a workforce operations and scheduling platform designed for Ontario long-term care and regulated healthcare environments. The platform helps organizations automate scheduling workflows, credential governance, staffing visibility, labor rule enforcement, and operational compliance.

Who is BaseCare designed for?

BaseCare is designed for long-term care homes, retirement communities, healthcare staffing teams, unions, healthcare operators, schedulers, HR departments, executive leadership teams, and regulated healthcare environments requiring workforce governance and compliance visibility.

Does BaseCare support unionized environments?

Yes. BaseCare is built with union-aware scheduling logic that supports seniority workflows, assignment rules, open shift bidding, overtime visibility, and workforce transparency. The platform is designed to reduce manual interpretation of collective agreements and improve auditability.

Can BaseCare prevent non-compliant staff from taking shifts?

Yes. BaseCare includes autonomous credential enforcement that can automatically block staff from accepting or being assigned shifts if required certifications, licenses, training records, or compliance documents are expired or missing.

What credentials can BaseCare track?

BaseCare can track CPR certifications, nursing licenses, vulnerable sector checks, onboarding documents, immunization records, training requirements, WSIB documentation, fit testing, mandatory education requirements, and organization-specific compliance workflows.

Can BaseCare reduce agency staffing dependency?

BaseCare helps organizations improve internal staffing visibility, optimize workforce utilization, accelerate open shift fulfillment, and improve workforce planning. These capabilities may reduce reliance on external staffing agencies over time.

How long does implementation take?

Implementation timelines vary based on organizational complexity, number of facilities, workforce structure, scheduling requirements, and integration scope. Most implementations begin with operational discovery, workflow mapping, configuration, and phased onboarding.

Does BaseCare integrate with existing systems?

BaseCare is designed to support integration workflows with HR systems, scheduling infrastructure, payroll systems, credential databases, staffing platforms, and workforce reporting environments depending on implementation requirements.

Is BaseCare cloud-based?

Yes. BaseCare is designed as a modern cloud-based workforce operations platform with secure remote access, workforce visibility, centralized management, and scalable deployment infrastructure.

Does BaseCare provide audit trails?

Yes. BaseCare maintains operational logs and workforce audit visibility across staffing actions, scheduling decisions, credential workflows, and compliance activities to improve transparency and inspection readiness.

Can staff access BaseCare on mobile devices?

Yes. Staff can access schedules, open shifts, notifications, credential updates, and workforce communication workflows through mobile-friendly access environments.

How does BaseCare support operational leadership?

BaseCare provides leadership teams with workforce dashboards, operational analytics, staffing visibility, compliance oversight, vacancy tracking, overtime exposure monitoring, and workforce reporting to support operational decision-making.
