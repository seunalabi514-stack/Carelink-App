Reducing Agency Spend by 35%

Real-world staffing optimization strategies for LTC operators managing labor pressure and compliance risk.

The Crisis of Reactive Staffing

In the current Ontario healthcare landscape, LTC operators are often forced into a cycle of "Reactive Staffing." A shift goes unfilled for two hours, the phone tree fails, and the facility is forced to call an agency at a 2.5x premium just to maintain minimum safe ratios.

Key Strategic Goal

To reduce agency spend, you must solve the Internal Fulfillment Gap—the time between a shift becoming vacant and the moment it is offered to every eligible internal staff member according to CBA rules.

1. Automating the Call-Out Logic

Traditional staffing clerks spend up to 4 hours a day on the phone. By the time they reach the 15th person on a seniority list, the first 5 have already made plans. BaseCare’s autonomous bidding engine sends simultaneous, seniority-weighted alerts.

2. Predictive Compliance

The second largest driver of agency spend isn't a lack of staff—it's staff who are ineligible due to expired credentials. By automating credential tracking, facilities ensure their full internal roster is "floor-ready" 100% of the time, eliminating last-minute surprises that trigger agency calls.

"We reclaimed $400k in our first year just by closing the 15-minute gap between a sick call and an internal broadcast."

— Director of Care, Region of Waterloo

Next Steps for Operators

Reducing spend by 35% is a logistical challenge, not a personnel one. It requires a shift toward autonomous systems that prioritize internal union staff with zero friction.
